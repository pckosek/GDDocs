# 7.2.1 Critical Path

## Classification

**Type:** Design / Engineering Guide

**Category:** Designing Worlds

**Difficulty:** Beginner

**Estimated Time:** 20–30 minutes

---

# Design Problem

Imagine entering a large dungeon.

Several corridors stretch into the darkness.

Some contain treasure.

Others contain enemies.

One eventually leads to the exit.

How should the player know where to go?

Should every path be equally important?

Should every room be required?

Good level design rarely places every location on the same level of importance.

Instead...

most games distinguish between the **Critical Path** and **Side Content**.

---

# Why This Matters

The Critical Path is the minimum sequence of actions required to complete the game or level.

Side Content consists of optional areas that reward curiosity, exploration, or mastery.

Together they create a balance between:

- guidance
- freedom
- exploration
- progression

Players always know how to continue...

...but they also have opportunities to discover more.

---

# Mental Model

Imagine reading a book.

The chapters tell the main story.

Footnotes provide additional information.

You can understand the book without reading every footnote...

...but reading them often enriches the experience.

Level design works similarly.

```text
Critical Path

↓

Complete The Level

-------------------------

Side Content

↓

Optional Rewards

↓

Return To Main Path
```

The player always has a clear way forward.

Exploration becomes a choice rather than a requirement.

---

# Example

Imagine a simple dungeon.

```text
Entrance

↓

Hallway

↓

Boss

↓

Exit
```

That is the Critical Path.

Now add:

```text
Hidden Room

↓

Treasure

↓

Return
```

or

```text
Puzzle Room

↓

Upgrade

↓

Return
```

These become Side Content.

The player chooses whether to explore them.

---

# Dissecting the Design

Notice that the level now serves two purposes.

The Critical Path answers:

> **How do I finish?**

The Side Content answers:

> **What happens if I become curious?**

Good levels often encourage both.

The player experiences guidance without losing the excitement of discovery.

---

# Design Principle

## Guide without restricting.

Players should rarely become completely lost.

At the same time...

they should feel rewarded for exploring beyond the obvious route.

Balancing the Critical Path with optional content creates worlds that feel both purposeful and alive.

---

# Practical Observation

Many beginning designers build levels as long corridors.

```text
Start

↓

Finish
```

While easy to understand...

these levels often provide few meaningful choices.

Adding carefully designed side paths allows players to:

- explore
- collect rewards
- experiment
- express curiosity

without preventing progress.

---

# Common Misconceptions

### "Every room should be required."

Not necessarily.

Optional areas often create memorable moments because players choose to discover them.

---

### "Side Content should be unimportant."

Not at all.

Optional content often contains:

- upgrades
- secrets
- story
- collectibles
- shortcuts

Players should feel rewarded for exploration.

---

### "Players should never become lost."

Small moments of uncertainty can encourage exploration.

The goal is not eliminating exploration.

The goal is preventing frustration.

---

### "The Critical Path must always be obvious."

Not always.

Some games intentionally challenge players to discover the correct route.

The appropriate level of guidance depends on the intended experience.
