# Design Problem

Suppose someone says:

> "I'm making a platformer."

That tells us something...

...but not very much.

Two platformers may feel completely different.

One focuses on:

- exploration

Another emphasizes:

- combat

A third rewards:

- puzzle solving

How can games that share the same genre create such different experiences?

The answer lies in their **Core Mechanics**.

---

# Why This Matters

Core mechanics are the fundamental actions available to the player.

They describe **what the player actually does**.

Almost every game is built by combining several mechanics together.

Understanding these building blocks helps designers create, analyze, and improve games.

---

# Mental Model

Think about an orchestra.

A genre is like the orchestra itself.

A platformer.

An RPG.

A strategy game.

Each orchestra creates a different kind of music.

But the orchestra is built from instruments.

```text
Genre

↓

Core Mechanics

↓

Player Experience
```

The instruments create the music.

The mechanics create the game.

# Common Core Mechanics

Some of the most common mechanics include:

| Mechanic | What the Player Does |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Move | Navigate the world |
| Jump | Traverse obstacles |
| Shoot / Attack | Deal damage |
| Collect | Gather items or resources |
| Interact | Activate objects or talk to characters |
| Build | Place or construct objects |
| Aim | Target attacks or abilities |
| Block / Defend | Reduce incoming damage |

# Design Principle

## Mechanics are the vocabulary of play.

Genres describe the overall experience.

Mechanics describe the individual actions that create that experience.

Designers build games by combining mechanics in thoughtful ways.

Changing even one mechanic can dramatically change how a game feels.

---

# Practical Observation

Beginning designers often start by choosing a genre.

Experienced designers often begin somewhere else.

They ask:

> **What should the player spend most of their time doing?**

The answer usually becomes the game's core mechanics.

Everything else supports those actions.

---

# Common Misconceptions

### "Every genre has its own mechanics."

Not necessarily.

Many mechanics appear across multiple genres.

Movement, collecting, and interaction are common examples.

---

### "More mechanics create a better game."

Usually not.

Many memorable games focus on only a small number of well-designed mechanics.

Adding mechanics increases complexity.

It does not automatically increase quality.

---

### "Mechanics determine graphics."

No.

Mechanics describe player actions.

Visual style is a separate design decision.

---

### "Mechanics and systems are the same."

Not quite.

Mechanics describe individual player actions.

Later we'll discover that systems connect many mechanics together.
